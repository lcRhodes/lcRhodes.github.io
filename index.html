Dr Luke C. Rhodes

I am a condensed matter physicist who's work bridges the divide between theory and experiment. My passion is in the understanding of correlated quantum materials, in particular unconventional superconductors, with the aim to enhance and stabilise these technologically important emerging technologies in more favourable regimes.

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher working at the University of St Andrews with Prof. Peter Wahl in the Centre for Designer Quantum Materials. We have been using ultra-low temperature Scanning Tunnelling Microscopy (STM) to uncover how electron-electron interactions stabilise emergent quantum properties of correlated materials and developed a numerical package to accurately simulate experimental STM signatures from models of the materials electronic structure.

I also have a PhD student (joint with Dr Bernd Braunecker) looking at identifying new forms of experimental signatures for the study of topology in condensed matter physics.

I finished my PhD in 2019 joint between Royal Holloway and the Diamond Light Source, working under Prof. Matthias Eschrig and Dr Timur Kim , where we combined electronic structure simulations and Angle Resolved Photoemission Spectroscopy measurements to understand superconductivity in the iron-based superconductors. Prior to this I completed a masters in Chemistry at the university of Sussex. My masters thesis was on the theoretical study of electron correlation in two-electron atoms supervised by Prof. Hazel Cox .
